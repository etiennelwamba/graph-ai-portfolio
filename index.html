About me

Turning complex evidence into useful decisions.

My background combines international development, evaluation, evidence synthesis and programme delivery across global and low- and middle-income country contexts.

Current focus

I am a Research Associate at the International Initiative for Impact Evaluation, contributing to systematic reviews and evidence gap maps, including literature searches, screening, critical appraisal, data extraction and evidence synthesis.

I use mixed quantitative and qualitative approaches, including meta-analysis, thematic synthesis and cost-effectiveness analysis, to support evidence-informed policy and practice.

About the course

No-code AI tools for responsible task automation.

The course is designed around applied, ethical productivity: from documents and websites to data analysis, chatbots, automation and research communication.

What I am building towards

The programme combines weekly live workshops, live help sessions, community forums, personalised feedback, capstone coaching and lifetime tutorial access. The final grade blends quizzes, workshop assignments, attendance, participation and a capstone project.
